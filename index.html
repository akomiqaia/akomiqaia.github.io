About Me

I'm Akaki Mikaia from Georgia. I was born 24 years ago under the sign of Leo. I grew up in Chkhorotsku* in a huge family. My family and rural environment helped me to develop a strong communal sense. It also taught me how to take care of my surroundings; How to grow different trees, vegetables or take care of animals. My life changed when I moved to Tbilisi (the capital city of Georgia) to study at university. In Tbilisi, I realized how technological and industrial development can break (or replaces) connections among people.


One night, just discussing the topic, my friends and I decided to start a project that would raise the community awareness in our hometown, especially among young people. The best way seemed to increase intellectual resources, so that we could introduce new and different ideas from all over the world. To acomplish that, we started a project to provide the local library with brand new books and other materials, given its poor situation at that time. We were buying books for the library every month. We, then, were presenting the new materials and organizing talks and discissions about them. All this evolved and became a platform to introduce new ideas to young people. The project has been a real communal achievement that united the people of the small town. Now, the project has grown and its got adopted by the neighbouring villages. That was the beginning of understanding my philosophy of life.

Prepare me - Scrum Master / Full Stack Developer

Prepare Me is a platform created for parents with kids with autism. On the platform parents are able to create printable books from templates to tell the social stories to their kids. Prepare me is a part of Tech for Better program at FAC where I took a role of SCRUM Master.

Bechdel and Beyond - DevOps/Full Stack Developer

Bechdel and Beyond is a platform that enables users to search for films and check if the movies pass the Bechdel test and alongside display gender parity for the crew and cast. Users are also able to submit their reviews and vote for the films.
